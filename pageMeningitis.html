More Info on Meningitis

It is a rare disease and is relatively short-term, as it resolves within days to weeks. Meningitis is usually caused by a viral infection but can also be bacterial or fungal.

How does it spread?

It can spread by airborne respiratory droplets (which is by coughing or sneezing).

Information on Treatments!

Most people, within two weeks, can recover on their own. But it can also become life-threatening, depending on the cause.

  • Ceftriaxone (Rocephin)
  • Meropenem
  • Cefotaxime (Claforan)
  • Cefepime (Maxipime)
  • Ceftazdime(Fortaz)
  • Ampicillin
  • Dexamethasone by injection
  • Hospitalization
  • Oxygen Therapy
