More Info on Chicken Pox

It is rare and highly contagious viral infection. It is preventable by vaccination and it causes a blister-like rash on skin. Chicken pox is short term (resolves within days to weeks). This disease is contagious to those who have not ever had chicken pox or have been vaccinated against it.

How does it spread?

It can spread by airborne respiratory droplets (which is by coughing or sneezing), by saliva, by skin-to-skin contact, by mother to baby by pregnancy, labor or nursing and by touching a contaminated surface.

Information on Treatments!

Some prescriptions you can take if you have chicken pox are:

  • Acyclovir (Zovirax)
  • Antihistamine (Benadryl)
  • Acetaminophen (Tylenol)
  • Oatmeal bath (soothing remedy)
  • Moisturizer (soothing remedy)
